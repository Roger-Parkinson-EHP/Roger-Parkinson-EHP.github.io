Books That Shaped My Thinking

These aren't just books I've read. They changed how I see systems, organizations, and reality itself.

The Advantage

Patrick Lencioni

Organizational health beats strategy every time. A dysfunctional team with a great strategy loses to a healthy team with an okay strategy. This book changed how I think about building teams.

Key insight: The smartest organization loses to a healthy one. Clarity and alignment beat brilliance.

The Goal

Eliyahu Goldratt

Theory of Constraints changed how I see bottlenecks. Every system has one constraint that limits throughput. Find it, exploit it, subordinate everything else to it.

Key insight: Optimizing a non-constraint is waste. Find the real bottleneck.

The Evolution of Everything

Matt Ridley

This book changed how I view reality. Bottom-up emergence beats top-down design. Evolution applies to technology, culture, economics, morality—everything.

Key insight: Complex systems emerge from simple rules and selection pressure. Design less, enable more.

How These Connect

These three books form a coherent worldview:

  • Lencioni: Healthy teams beat smart teams
  • Goldratt: Focus on constraints, not everything
  • Ridley: Let systems evolve, don't over-engineer

I bring this thinking to every system I build. Health. Constraints. Emergence.
