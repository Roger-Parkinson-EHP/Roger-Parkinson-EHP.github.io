## Notable Findings

### Family with 30 Children
Thomas + Agnes Wells, married 1515 in Somerset, England — **30 documented children.** (Runner-up: 29 children, married 1444 in Herefordshire.)

### Connection to Royalty
- **Frederik I of Denmark** (b. 1471, Haderslev) — appears in the tree
- **Margaret of Denmark** (b. 1456, Copenhagen, d. 1486, Stirling Castle, Scotland)
- **Sancho II** (b. 1110, Navarra) — medieval Iberian royalty

### Earliest Documented Ancestor
**Margred** — born approximately 150 AD in Wales. FamilySearch ID: 93QN-C2D.

### The Pendle Connection
Roger's paternal line (Parkinson) comes from **Newchurch in Pendle, Lancashire** — the exact village at the center of the infamous **Pendle Witch Trials of 1612.** His ancestor James Parkinson (b. 1733) was from "Foothouse Gate, Newchurch in Pendle."

## What This Means for Ancestral Fire

This dataset is a **ready-made prototype**:

1. **72,182 people** with structured data (names, dates, places)
2. **100% FamilySearch linkage** — every person can be cross-referenced
3. **1,800+ years** of documented history
4. **Multiple countries** — England, Wales, Scotland, Ireland, Sweden, Denmark, France, Germany, Netherlands
5. **Rich migration stories** — European origins → American settlement
6. **Historical event connections** — Revolution, Civil War, Pioneer era, Witch Trials, Famine

**The question isn't whether we have data. It's whether we can make 72,000 names feel like 72,000 stories.**
