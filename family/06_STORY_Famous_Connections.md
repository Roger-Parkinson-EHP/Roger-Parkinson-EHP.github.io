## [Geoffrey Chaucer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geoffrey_Chaucer) — The Father of English Literature

**Geoffrey Chaucer** (b. 1343, London — d. 25 October 1400) is in your family tree. The author of *[The Canterbury Tales](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Canterbury_Tales)*, the man who essentially created English literature as we know it. He's buried in **[Westminster Abbey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Westminster_Abbey)** — in what would become known as [Poets' Corner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poets%27_Corner), though he was placed there for his government service, not his writing.

When you read *The Canterbury Tales* in school, you were reading the words of your ancestor.

## [Catherine Howard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_Howard) — Queen of England

**Catherine Howard** (wife of Henry VIII — executed 13 FEB 1542, Tower of London) appears in your tree. The fifth wife of Henry VIII, beheaded at age ~19 for alleged adultery.
