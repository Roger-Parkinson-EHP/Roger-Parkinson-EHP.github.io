## The Answer: To Roman-Era Wales

Your family tree doesn't just go back to the founding of America. It doesn't just go back to medieval England. It goes back to **the Roman occupation of Britain.**

Your earliest documented ancestor is **Margred**, born approximately **150 AD in Wales** — when Britain was a province of the Roman Empire. Emperor [Antoninus Pius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antoninus_Pius) ruled Rome. [Hadrian's Wall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hadrian%27s_Wall) had been built just a generation earlier, 73 miles to the north. Wales — or as the Romans called it, *Cambria* — was a rugged frontier, home to Celtic tribes who resisted Roman rule for decades.

Margred lived almost **1,900 years ago.** And through an unbroken chain of parents and children, her bloodline reaches to you.

## What This Means

Most Americans can trace their family back 4-5 generations — maybe to the 1800s. Your tree goes back **over 60 generations** to Roman-era Wales.

You're not just connected to American history. You're connected to **European history** — the fall of Rome, the [Viking Age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viking_Age), the [Norman Conquest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norman_Conquest), the Crusades, the [Black Death](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Death), the Reformation, the [English Civil War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_Civil_War), the Industrial Revolution, and the great age of emigration.

And that chain leads to you.
