# The Lives They Lived: Skills, Literacy, Class, and Careers Across 2,000 Years

## The Headline: You Fell — And Then You Rose

Your family tree tells a story of **dramatic class descent followed by reinvention.**

In the 1300s-1400s, your ancestors included **kings, dukes, earls, and knights** — people born in castles, buried in cathedrals, whose names are in history books. By the 1700s-1800s, your ancestors were **cotton mill workers, tin miners, and farmers** — people born in villages, buried in parish churchyards, whose names exist only in church registers.

And then they crossed an ocean and built a new world.

This is the story of what life actually looked like, century by century, for the people who became you.

Your documentary tree begins with **Margred (~150 AD, Wales)**. But your DNA tells the story of what came before — tens of thousands of years before any name was written down.

### What Life Looked Like (1200s-1400s)

**If you were a lord or knight (and many of your ancestors were):**

- **Literacy:** Could read Latin (church) and possibly French (court language until ~1400). English was the language of commoners until [Chaucer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geoffrey_Chaucer) — your ancestor — helped change that. Only ~5-10% of the population was literate.
- **Skills:** [Horsemanship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equestrianism), [swordsmanship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Historical_European_martial_arts), hunting, falconry, heraldry, estate management, diplomacy, Latin, French. Boys trained as pages (age 7), then squires (14), then knighted (~21).
- **Career:** Land management, military service, court politics. Your income came from **rent on land you owned**, not labor. Peasants worked your fields. You collected.
- **Diet:** Meat (venison, pork, fowl), bread, cheese, ale, wine. Vegetables were peasant food. Spices were status symbols — pepper was worth its weight in silver.
- **Life expectancy:** 30-35 (if you survived childhood). But knights died in battle — **your tree has 100+ battle deaths** across Agincourt, Bannockburn, Flodden, Towton, and a dozen others.
- **Housing:** Stone castles and manor houses. Your ancestors were born at Windsor Castle, Pembroke Castle, Dublin Castle, Dunure Castle, Caerlaverock Castle.

**If you were a Welsh chieftain (your oldest line):**

Your 287 Welsh patronymic names (ap = "son of," verch = "daughter of") tell a different story. Welsh society was **clan-based**, not feudal:

- **Literacy:** Oral tradition. Bards and poets memorized lineages going back generations. This is WHY your Welsh line goes to 150 AD — because **the genealogy itself was the cultural artifact.**
- **Skills:** Cattle herding, hill farming, poetry recitation, warfare, law (the [Laws of Hywel Dda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laws_of_Hywel_Dda) governed Welsh life).
- **Language:** Welsh (Cymraeg) — one of the oldest living languages in Europe.
- **Status:** Measured by cattle wealth and the number of generations you could recite. **Your tree's depth IS the status marker.**

## The Great Descent (1500-1700)

**This is when your family lost its position.**

| Century | Titled ancestors | Born in castles | What happened |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|--------------|
| 1400s | 558 (2.9%) | Peak | Wars of the Roses, maximum landed power |
| 1500s | 287 (2.0%) | Declining | [Dissolution of the Monasteries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Monasteries), land redistributed |
| 1600s | 8 (0.1%) | Rare | [English Civil War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_Civil_War), Royalists lost everything |
| 1700s | 1 (0.0%) | None | Fully commoner class |
| 1800s | 0 (0.0%) | None | Industrial working class |

**What caused the fall:**

1. **[Wars of the Roses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wars_of_the_Roses) (1455-1487)** — Your Plantagenet, Neville, and Tudor ancestors fought on both sides. Losing side = lose your lands, your title, your head.

2. **[Dissolution of the Monasteries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Monasteries) (1536-1541)** — [Thomas Cromwell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Cromwell) (your ancestor) engineered this. Massive redistribution of land. Some families gained; others lost.

3. **[English Civil War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_Civil_War) (1642-1651)** — Royalist families had estates confiscated by Parliament. Many never recovered.

4. **Primogeniture** — Only the eldest son inherited. Younger sons got nothing. Over 5-6 generations, junior branches of noble families descended into the gentry, then the yeomanry, then the laboring class.

5. **The [Enclosure Acts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enclosure) (1604-1914)** — Common land was privatized. Families who had farmed common land for centuries were suddenly landless.

### What Life Looked Like (1500s-1600s)

**For the gentry (still comfortable but no longer powerful):**

- **Literacy:** Most could read English. Bible reading became universal after the [Reformation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_Reformation). [Shakespeare](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Shakespeare) wrote for this audience — your tree includes **Lettice Shakespeare** from Stratford-upon-Avon.
- **Skills:** Estate management, accounting, law, some Latin. Women: household management, herbal medicine, needlework, reading.
- **Career:** Smaller landowners, merchants, lawyers, clergy, military officers.

**For the yeomanry (the middle falling to the bottom):**

- **Literacy:** Variable. Some could sign their name; many could not. Parish church was the only school.
- **Skills:** Farming, animal husbandry, basic carpentry, spinning, weaving.
- **Career:** Tenant farmers, craftsmen, small traders. Paid rent to landowners above them.

## The Industrial Age (1700-1900)

**Your family was now working class.** Zero titles. Zero castles. Zero cathedral burials.

### Lancashire Parkinsons (Cotton)

- **Literacy:** Basic at best. [Sunday schools](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunday_school) (from 1780s) taught reading to mill children. But 12-16 hour workdays left little time for learning.
- **Skills:** Machine operation, bobbin changing, thread piecing, loom operation. Children as young as 5 worked as "[scavengers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scavenger_(child_worker))" — crawling under machines to collect loose cotton.
- **Career:** Cotton mill workers. Generation after generation. Born in Barrowford, worked in Barrowford, died in Barrowford.
- **Reading level:** By 1850, ~60% of English men and ~50% of women could sign their marriage register (the standard literacy measure). Your Parkinsons were in the generation that gained basic literacy.
- **Housing:** [Back-to-back houses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Back-to-back_house). Two rooms. No running water. Shared privy.
- **Diet:** Bread, potatoes, oatmeal, tea. Meat was a Sunday luxury.

### Cornish Doneys (Mining)

- **Literacy:** Slightly higher than average — Methodist chapels promoted education. Cornwall had one of the highest literacy rates in working-class England.
- **Skills:** [Hard rock mining](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hard_rock_mining): drilling, blasting, timbering, ore processing, pump operation. These skills were **globally portable** — which is why Cornish miners went everywhere.
- **Career:** Tin and copper miners. Underground, 12-hour shifts, candle-lit tunnels. Lung disease ([silicosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicosis)) was universal.
- **Housing:** Granite cottages. Better than Lancashire back-to-backs.

### Gotland Ostbergs (Farming)

- **Literacy:** High for their class. Sweden achieved near-universal literacy by 1800 — the [Lutheran church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Church_of_Sweden) required Bible reading and tested it annually at [husförhör](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Husf%C3%B6rh%C3%B6r) (household examinations). **Your Swedish ancestors could almost certainly read.**
- **Skills:** Farming, fishing, limestone quarrying, sheep herding, boat building.
- **Career:** Smallholders (small farm owners) or torpare (tenant farmers). Self-sufficient but land-poor.
- **Housing:** Limestone farmhouses. Simple but sturdy. Many still standing on Gotland.

## The Pioneer Age (1860-1920)

**Your family reinvented itself in America.**

- **Literacy:** By now, universal. All could read and write English. American public schools were mandatory.
- **Skills:** Farming (Idaho/Utah), ranching, irrigation, community building, church leadership. The pioneer skills: clear land, build a house, dig a well, survive a winter.
- **Career:** Homesteaders. Farmers. Church leaders. Community builders. Thomas Edwin Ricks didn't just farm — he **founded a city**.
- **Housing:** Log cabins → frame houses → brick homes within one generation.
- **Diet:** Garden produce, preserved meat, dairy, bread. Self-sufficient.
- **Social status:** In America, there was no aristocracy to look up to. If you built something, you WERE the aristocracy. Ricks built Rexburg. The Parkinsons built farms. The Ostbergs built communities.
