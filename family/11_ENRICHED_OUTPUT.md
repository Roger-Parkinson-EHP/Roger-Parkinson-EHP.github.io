## What Just Happened

For each ancestor above, the enrichment pipeline:

1. **Queried the database** — found the ancestor record with name, dates, places, FamilySearch ID
2. **Hit Wikipedia** — fetched articles about their birthplace, death place, and the person themselves
3. **Hit Wikidata** — got structured facts: coordinates, population, images, historical events
4. **Searched YouTube** — found relevant documentaries, walking tours, historical content
5. **Generated this output** — combining all sources into a rich, shareable narrative

**Total API cost: $0.** All sources are free.

**In the real product**, an LLM (Claude) would take all this raw enrichment data and weave it into
a compelling narrative — not a data dump, but a story. The kind of story you'd read to your
grandchildren. The kind you'd share on Facebook. The kind that makes you say:
