## Key Findings

- **72,182 individuals** with **100% FamilySearch linkage**
- **25 generations deep** — 33,227 unique direct ancestors of Roger Parkinson
- Ancestors back to **~150 AD in Wales**
- **Geoffrey Chaucer**, **3 Kings of England**, **Thomas Cromwell**, **Catherine Howard** in the tree
- **Thomas Edwin Ricks** (direct ancestor) **founded Rexburg, Idaho** — Roger's birthplace
- **69 ancestors buried at Westminster Abbey**
- **6 ancestors killed at Agincourt** (1415)
- Two great migration stories: **Lancashire → Idaho** and **Gotland, Sweden → Idaho**
- All enrichment APIs are **free** ($0/month for prototype)
- **23andMe DNA** independently validates the documentary tree — every component matches
- **Paternal haplogroup R-S476** traces to Yamnaya steppe culture, connected to Niall of the Nine Hostages
- **Maternal haplogroup V** shares lineage with Benjamin Franklin, originated in post-Ice Age Iberia

## What These Prototypes Prove

1. **The data exists** — 72K ancestors with structured data ready for enrichment
2. **The stories are compelling** — not just dates and names, but narratives people would share
3. **The enrichment pipeline works** — Wikidata/Wikipedia/YouTube add real depth
4. **The APIs are free** — zero cost to build the prototype
5. **This is what nobody else is doing** — no genealogy tool turns data into stories like this
6. **DNA bridges the gap** — where records end (~150 AD), DNA extends to 275,000 years ago
7. **The three-layer architecture works** — DNA + Records + Enrichment = complete human story
8. **Direct links to content** — not just summaries, but clickable links to Wikipedia articles, YouTube documentaries, and FamilySearch records people would never find on their own
