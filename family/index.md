# The Parkinson Family Story

**72,182 ancestors. 1,800 years. 15+ countries. One unbroken chain.**

From **Margred** in Roman-era Wales (~150 AD) to **Roger Parkinson** in Rexburg, Idaho (1975) — this is the story of where we came from. DNA extends the story another 275,000 years, to the first humans in Eastern Africa.

*Everything below is documented. Every name is real. Every [FamilySearch](https://www.familysearch.org) link goes to a live record.*

- The family tree spans **15+ countries**: England, Scotland, Wales, France, Sweden, Germany, Netherlands, Ireland, Italy, Spain, Denmark, Norway, Belgium, Hungary, Russia

## The Migration Map

Every branch of this family crossed an ocean. Here's why:

| Push Factor | Where | When | Who |
|-------------|-------|------|-----|
| Religious persecution | East England | 1620-1640 | 1,595 to Massachusetts, 830 to Connecticut |
| Scottish border wars | Scottish Borders | 1300-1600 | Scotts, Maxwells, Turnbulls, Stewarts |
| Industrial Revolution | Lancashire | 1760-1900 | Parkinsons (cotton mills) |
| Mining collapse | Cornwall | 1830-1900 | Doneys (tin and copper) |
| Crop failure + class rigidity | Gotland, Sweden | 1850-1910 | Ostbergs |
| Highland Clearances | Scotland | 1750-1860 | Kerrs, Smiths, McKinlays |
| Religious recruitment | Scandinavia | 1870-1890 | LDS converts to Utah/Idaho |

All waves converged in one place: **southeastern Idaho.**
