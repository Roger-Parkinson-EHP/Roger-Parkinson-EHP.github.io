Enterprise experience

I've spent most of my career inside large, regulated financial institutions. I know what it takes to change them.

Bank of America (2010-2016)

I was recruited by the COO to fix process and technology gaps during the mortgage crisis. We had a million loans that needed modification under federal mandate and the systems couldn't keep up.

I led a 60-person team. We unified 47 separate data sources into one workflow system that served 12,000 associates. Federal compliance went from 70% to 95% in 30 days. That eliminated the Matters Requiring Attention from regulators and avoided $50M+ in potential penalties.

The data precision and audit trail I built contributed to a class action lawsuit dismissal. I've sat in depositions defending that work.

Wells Fargo (2019-2022)

Credit risk data operations. I built a DataOps platform from scratch using GitHub, APIs, and metadata-driven architecture. A small team produced the output that previously required 60 people.

We collapsed 8-week reporting cycles to daily at 99.8% accuracy, with full audit compliance across 22,000+ daily ELT transactions. The framework became a template for other transformations at Wells and was still in use years after I left.

GE and Six Sigma (2000-2017)

Six Sigma Master Black Belt. Hundreds of process improvements across 20+ business units at GE, Bank of America, and Vacasa. This is where I learned to look at a system, find the constraint, and fix it systematically.
